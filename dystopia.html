Utopia/ Distopia

Cultural Customization

In 1950, thirty per cent of the world’s population was urban, and by 2050 this number is projected to change to sixty six per cent. To house this expected urbanised growth the world has forecast 41 mega-cities with more than 10 million inhabitants by 2030 across the globe. How countries deal with this expansion through the cities’ construction, environmental impact and integrated infrastructure will have great consequences for the future of the planet. While these mega-cities are being developed, both rural settlements and existing cities in decline over the globe are being lost over the decades to conflict, neglect and depopulation. As people relocate to key mega cities, culture, heritage and communities disappear along with them. Homes hold memories of people, places and events. These are lost as homes are abandoned or soullessly replaced. What if Smart construction technology can be used to collect physical data from existing remarkable structures unique to the region which are at risk to loss and embed them into a customizable, modern city grid? Latest 3D scanning, documenting, BIM and robotic assembly technology can be utilised on a mass scale as basis for renovating and incorporating cultural structures into modern and comfortable homes. Modern prefabricated amenities and smart systems along with highly energy efficient envelopes can be integrated with the grid, services and local architectural assets. The culturally rich assets are revived through digital and traditional means to emphasis the cultural value and beauty within a new urban utopia. The use of the city’s Digital twin and BIM processes extend and improve the real world through mass customization and cultural integration. By bringing to the forefront forgotten structures and architecture which emphasize regional context and material, the vision works towards recovering and emphasizing the local vernacular and culture to be reinterpreted for the 21st century.
